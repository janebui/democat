Voting Information

Register to Vote

In California, you can easily Register to Vote Online

You must be:

  • A United States citizen and a resident of California
  • 18 years old or older on Election Day
  • Not currently in state or federal prison, or on parole for the conviction of a felony
  • Not prohibited from voting by court because of mental incompetency

You will need:

  • Your California driver license or California identification card number
  • Last four digits of your social security number
  • Your date of birth

Vote by Mail

Due to COVID-19 this year, all registered voters in CA will automatically receive a mail-in ballot in the mail. Voters may also choose to vote in-person at polling locations if they wish.

Vote-by-mail ballots that are mailed must be postmarked on or before Election Day and received by your county elections office no later than 3 days after Election Day.

Vote in Person

Although we encourage you to vote by mail this year because of COVID-19, you may also choose to vote in-person on Election Day. Find your local polling place here.

Some polling locations also offer early voting, where you can go in-person before Election Day to fill out a ballot, or drop off your mail-in ballot. Find out if there is an early voting location near you.
